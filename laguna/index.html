June 4, 2000
Wipeout King Van Fossen Defends Title At Laguna Seca

MONTEREY (AvantNews) - Local squid favorite, heavyweight bruiser, and 1980s retro-sportbike devotee Andy Van Fossen met fans' expectations at world-renowned Laguna Seca Raceway last Thursday as he managed a low-speed, single-vehicle crash in the paddock on his Honda CBR-1000 Hurricane motorcycle.

Of the dozens of eyewitnesses recounting the incident to reporters, few agreed on the exact circumstances. "I think he highsided it, but at two miles an hour what's the difference?" queried Team Yamaha Retail rider David Johnson. "He just fell off," commented onlooker and Honda Sport Tourer Daniel Pifko. Added Michael "Hi-Viz" Tsao, "The instructor said to hit the brakes hard, but I don't remember anyone saying throw yourself off the damn bike."

An accident reconstruction expert on the scene was unable to shed further light on Van Fossen's separation from his bike. "All I can say for sure is that this motorcycle has been through a lot," said AFM 650 Twins Champ Dan Argano. "Judging from the extensive recent damage to the bike, my best guess is Andy hit a deer or possibly a California Gray Whale in the pit area. But how could such a big animal make it across the track and into the paddock without being seen by anyone?"

Thursday's turn of events further increases Van Fossen's commanding lead in the 2000 AvantGo Motorcycle Get-Off Championship standings. With 79 points, Van Fossen easily outpaces second-place Tsao, who currently holds a total of one point for his February parking lot fallover.

AvantGo Employees Humiliated By Irrefutable Truth of Videotape

MONTEREY (AvantNews) - After nearly a full day of telling their coworkers high-speed knee-draggin', shit-kickin', death-defyin' stories of the previous day at Laguna Seca Raceway, certain AvantGo employees were shocked and silenced into abject humility after viewing an eight-millimeter Sony Handycam videotape of their track day.

Andy Van Fossen, Michael Tsao, David Johnson, and Daniel Pifko huddled quietly in AvantTheater and noted the wussiness of their straightaway speeds, their very-nearly-off-perpendicular lean angles through turn 2, the approximately two-foot distance between knee and pavement through turn 11, and the wide, sweeping scenic route taken through the Corkscrew and Rainey Curve.

The riders have declined to comment further on the videotape. 

 Rift in Space-Time Continuum Captures, Releases AvantGo Employees

MARINA, CALIFORNIA (AvantNews) - Dr. Emmett "Doc" L. Brown's worst fears were realized Wednesday when the very fabric of space and time ripped and swallowed up several AvantGo employees, only to release them hours later, apparently unharmed and without any known Karmic imbalance.

The strange chain of events began when Mike Tsao and Andy Van Fossen easily made several normally impossible left turns into traffic while driving Tom Whittaker's truck that was towing a rickety garden cart. "It was just like the Red Sea parted in front of us. I mean, if you believe in that Bible stuff," said Van Fossen. "And it happened on Ralston Avenue, not in Egypt. And I don't think they had U-Haul 2,000 years ago."

Further weirdness ensued as Tsao, Van Fossen, Daniel Pifko, David Johnson, and Johnson's friends Mike and Jason entered a bar that was evidently teleported from the late-1980s David Lynch television series Twin Peaks. Tsao disappeared into a neighboring cardroom, only to reappear half an hour later carrying $94.00, twenty dollars' worth of food stamps, most of a loaf of Velveeta, an unopened "Value-Pak" of Huggies diapers, and a lawn gnome. Several unidentified patrons of the bar marked his return with a horrifying karaoke medley of Queen's Bohemian Rhapsody, Lionel Ritchie's Hello, and countless Elton John works.

It was not until pointed investigation by Pifko that the group confirmed that it was anywhere but on Planet Earth in the current Universe. Pifko engaged the drunkest and wobbliest barfly in conversation only to learn that the individual, one Rob Scott, was in fact a member of the SyncML team at IBM Corporation, specifically in charge of SyncML's relationship with the IBM DB2 Anywhere project. The two exchanged contact information, and Scott expressed interest in an employment position at AvantGo. Shortly thereafter Pifko's head suddenly exploded.

At approximately 1:00 a.m. the rift in the Universe closed and expelled the group into the local Denny's restaurant, where they enjoyed a completely typical, boisterous, post-drinking Multiple Slam meal. No evidence of the earlier oddities manifested itself with the exception of the recovering Pifko, who extracted two perfectly round over-easy egg yolks from his entree and ate them last.
